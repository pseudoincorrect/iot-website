  • 2015

    Embedded Systems Engineer

    Lorraine Research Laboratory in Computer Science and its Applications – LORIA (INRIA, CNRS, Université de Lorraine), Nancy, France



    After finishing my internship and validation my MS degree, I continued to work as Embedded Systems Engineer for another year and a half. I worked mainly on a rat brain implant device that would allow our team to record the neural signal of rat during a set of experimentation. In parallel we worked on the optimization of a compression algorithm (named FBAR) needed to increase the efficiency of our data link. When compressing and sorting the neural spiking data we can acquire more neural channels (points of acquisition in the brain). This required a heavy refactoring on our system by optimizing both the overall compression algorithm and optimizing the code itself in order to be able to run on a constrained system (microcontroller).

  • 2014

    Master of Sciences in System Control

    Ecole Supérieure des Sciences et Technologies de l'Ingénieur de Nancy (ESSTIN), Nancy, France



    The ESSTIN is what we call in France a “Grande Ecole d’ingénieur” (Literally “Great School of Engineers”), a school dedicated to form engineer on various subjects. The admission is based on a qualification examination. Then the students spend 5 years being trained on various area from abstract subjects such as Algebra or Analytical Mechanics to applied sciences such a product conception or software design, but also on management and secondarily on literature and human sciences. The specification I chose was Systems and Control, a subject where mathematics, hardware and computer sciences are merging. This diploma is equivalent and recognized as a Master of Science degree (MS).
